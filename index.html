Ahmed K. Hafsi

Negotiation · Game Theory · Strategy · APAC (Singapore)

I turn negotiation into a system: the leverage, incentives, and terms that decide who captures value, made explicit and engineered.

I build negotiation operating models that turn scattered deal-making into a disciplined capability, and I lead high-stakes negotiations where the result has to show up in the P&L. The work blends game theory, behavioral science, and hard data with operational reality, from factory-floor constraints to boardroom approvals.

My focus is industrials across the Asia-Pacific, from automotive and semiconductors to consumer tech and chemicals. Singapore is my launch pad, and I work all over the region, from Japan to Indonesia. I do this today at Infineon, and built the same capability for Dyson before that.

What I bring

Deal architecture. Pricing, indexation, and terms that hold value over years, not just on signing day.

Leverage and incentives. Game-theoretic analysis of who really holds power, and why.

Negotiation operating models. Turning ad-hoc deal-making into a repeatable, governed capability.

Finance-validated value. Outcomes measured in gross margin, EBITDA, and cash.
